Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is the "1/4 inch rule" regarding hardwood floor installation in Batesville, IN?

The 1/4 inch rule refers to the expansion gap required between the wood planks and the wall. Because wood is a natural material that expands and contracts with the local humidity in Batesville, IN, this gap prevents the floor from buckling or warping over time.

2. How do you handle old material disposal during a flooring renovation?

We manage the entire removal and disposal process. Whether it is old carpet, tack strips, or broken tiles, our team hauls away all debris to ensure your property is left clean and ready for immediate use after the new flooring installation.

3. Is an epoxy floor better than tile for a commercial kitchen?

For commercial kitchens, epoxy is often preferred because it is seamless and non-porous. Unlike tile, there are no grout lines where bacteria or grease can hide, making it much easier to pass health inspections in Batesville, IN.

4. Can you install new flooring over my existing tile or wood?

In many cases, yes. Our technicians in Batesville, IN will assess the levelness and stability of your current subfloor to determine if a floating floor like laminate or LVP can be installed directly over it.

5. Is your industrial carpet cleaning available for after-hours service?

We understand that businesses in Batesville, IN cannot afford downtime, so we offer flexible scheduling, including evenings and weekends, to ensure your operations remain uninterrupted.

6. What is the difference between refinishing and resurfacing a floor?

Refinishing usually involves sanding down to the raw wood and applying a new stain, while hardwood floor resurfacing is a lighter process that refreshes the top coat. We can help you decide which is right for your home in Batesville, IN.
